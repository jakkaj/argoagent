Bendigo is a city located in north-central Victoria, Australia, roughly 150km north-west of Melbourne. With a population of 103,818 as of 2022, Bendigo is Australia's 19th-largest city. It is the fourth-largest inland city in the country and the fourth most populous in Victoria. The city is administered by the City of Greater Bendigo, surrounded by smaller towns like Castlemaine and Maryborough. The traditional owners of the land are the Dja Dja Wurrung people. Bendigo was transformed into a major gold-mining economy in the 1850s, a boom that lasted until the early 20th century. As of the 21st century, Bendigo has seen considerable growth, becoming one of the fastest-growing regional centres in Victoria, particularly following the completion of the Calder Freeway in 2009.
